Database 1

    What are primary keys?

  • They are some unique identifier for each row.

    • What are foreign keys?

  • They still uniquely identify each row, but they originated from a different table.

    • What is the difference between a tuple and an attribute?

  • Tuples are entire rows of data. Attributes are the name of each column and describe what is in it.

    • What is SQL? (What does it stand for and what does it do?)

  • It stands for "Structured Query Language" and is great for grouping short lines of code together to organize raw data into tables.

    • Provide an example of something that could be used as a primary key and explain why you chose that.

  • A fingerprint could be considered a primary key because it is unique to every person and can be used to differentiate people.
