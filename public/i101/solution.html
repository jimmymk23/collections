Our Solution

To tackle our problem of trying to get our customers more connected with our food truck, we are going to implement a few things into an app and/or website. Our solution is to help target those on a college campus within a 5 mile radius of a food truck. We feel that it is important that consumers are able to properly locate the food truck and order food through an app to skip wait times, if on a time crunch.

The Map

The first of which is a map that will show the live GPS location of our truck. This can be done fairly easily by purchasing a GPS tracking device and installing it to an internet-connected device. This can keep a log of your frequent locations on the manufacturer’s servers and share that information with you, allowing you to create a feed of your current location on your website. This would be the most efficient and convenient way for your customers to stay up-to-date with your location in case you are running late or made a last-minute change to your schedule. Users would also be able to see the status of the truck, such as “traveling”, “set up”, and/or other customizable messages to let users know if the truck is currently moving or not, so they can wait until the truck arrives at its destination.

The Schedule

Of cource we would also offer a tradition schedule for those who would like to see where we plan to be tomorrow, the next week, and possibly the next month depending on how extensive the planning is. Though sometimes, events occur that are out of our control, such as weather, road construction, or even a breakdown that can delay or completely hinder our previously set schedule. One remedy to fix the rigid schedule problem is to embed a Google Calendar into the website. This will allow the owner to simply open the google calendar app on their phone, make a few location changes to their schedule, and hit done; the updating happens all behind the scenes, making changes live instantly on the website and app for the public.

Accounts

In order to offer a reward point system to our users, they will need to have individualized accounts. Within their account, they will be assigned a unique id, their name will be recorded, an email and password will be kept, the amount of reward points earned, and their favorite item will all be kept on file.

In this database, we are utilizing both primary keys, to a differentiate each user, and foreign keys, which reference the primary key from the menu table.
